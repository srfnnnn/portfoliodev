Concept and Purpose


The idea behind this AR name card was to create a professional yet minimal identity that communicates essential information instantly, while also demonstrating my skills in technology and immersive media.

Instead of a conventional business card, this design serves as a conversation starter, an interactive showcase of my technical skills and a strong personal brand representation for clients. The AR component adds an immersive layer, transforming the card into a modern and engaging experience.

Logo and Branding Concept


The logo for “Sorfina & Grid” was designed to reflect both my identity and my connection to technology.

  • “Sorfina”- derived from my name, making the brand personal and authentic
  • “Grid” - structure, alignment and digital workflows

The Sorfina & Grid logo is designed as a minimalist monogram, combining selected letterforms from the brand name into a single, cohesive mark. The overlapping typography represents the fusion of personal identity (“Sorfina”) and structured design thinking (“Grid”).

The interlocking letters symbolise connection, balance and integration — reflecting how creativity and structure work together in my design process. While the forms appear fluid and organic, they are intentionally aligned in a vertical composition, referencing the idea of a design grid, which is fundamental in digital and graphic design.

Additional design


— The addition of the blue molecular structure reinforces the brand’s connection to technology, development and innovation. Inspired by molecular and network diagrams, the interconnected nodes represent how ideas, skills and technologies come together to form meaningful digital solutions. This visual also reflects areas such as web development, 3D/VR development and interactive systems, where structure and connectivity are essential.


AR Integration and Experience


The physical card acts as an AR marker, which triggers digital content when scanned. This transforms the card from a static object into an interactive experience, allowing viewers to access additional information or visuals beyond what is printed.

  • Unity Hub

    • Used as the main development environment to build and manage the AR experience. Unity was responsible for setting up the scene, handling the AR interaction and ensuring that digital elements are accurately triggered when the physical name card is scanned.

  • Unity Asset Store

    • Assets from the Unity Asset Store were used to support the AR experience, such as 3D elements or supporting resources. This allowed for efficient development while maintaining visual quality and consistency.

  • Canva

    • Canva was used to design the visual layout of the physical name card, including typography, colour scheme and overall branding. This ensured a clean, minimalist design before integrating it into the AR workflow.

Name Card Designs


The colour contrast plays an important role in the overall composition. The warm, neutral background conveys professionalism and approachability, while the cool blue molecular graphic introduces a modern, tech-driven feel. This contrast highlights the harmony between human creativity and technical precision.

The name card layout is intentionally clean and well-structured. Personal details are clearly presented on one side for readability, while the logo and brand identity are given space to stand out. This ensures the card remains both functional and visually impactful, suitable for professional networking in creative and tech-related fields.
