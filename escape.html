One of Us Has a Secret


In this futuristic VR escape experience, players navigate through four interactive puzzle rooms designed to test intelligence, memory and problem-solving skills. As the environment reacts in real time, only quick thinkers can unlock the final door and escape before time runs out.


Introduction

One of Us Has a Secret is a thriller-mystery VR escape room set in Valley High School, where players take on the role of Vero Miler, a top student who has been falsely accused of hacking into the teachers’ files. Trapped in detention and racing against time before the principal returns, the player must follow a series of hidden clues left behind by the real prankster to prove their innocence.

Project Context

One of Us Has a Secret is a school VR game design project focused on creating a narrative-driven escape room experience. The objective was to design a suspenseful yet school-friendly thriller that relies on environmental storytelling, puzzle logic and time-based pressure rather than violence.

The project challenges players to think critically under time constraints while navigating multiple puzzle rooms through VR teleportation mechanics. Inspired by the novel One of Us Is Lying, the experience explores themes of false accusations, secrecy and psychological tension. The project was developed with a strong emphasis on puzzle clarity, atmosphere and immersive storytelling within a virtual reality environment.


Story and Narrative

The player takes on the role of Vero Miler, a quiet but high-achieving student at Valley High School who is suddenly accused of hacking into the teachers’ files. Despite her strong reputation and technical skills, her name appears in the system’s login history and no one believes her claims of innocence.

Locked alone in detention, Vero discovers a mysterious note challenging her to follow a trail of hidden clues scattered throughout the school. As she progresses through abandoned hallways, empty classrooms, the library and the staff room, an unseen prankster mocks her every move while the clock counts down.

Although the player uncovers a timeline that suggests the truth, the final revelation delivers a psychological twist — evidence alone is meaningless if no one is willing to listen. The experience ends on a dark cliffhanger, reinforcing the themes of manipulation, injustice and unresolved truth.

Puzzle Design Approach

Each puzzle was designed to test a different skill, including logic, observation, memory and pattern recognition. The puzzles gradually increase in complexity to match the rising tension of the story.

  1. The Locked Locker – logic and numerical reasoning
  2. The Empty Room – observation and sequence recognition
  3. The Secret Code – pattern recognition and colour association
  4. The Final Truth – deduction and timeline analysis

All puzzles are directly tied to the narrative, reinforcing the idea that the player is uncovering fragments of the truth left behind by the prankster.


Visual and Environmental Design

The art direction focuses on creating an eerie, unsettling atmosphere while remaining believable within a school environment. Lighting plays a key role in guiding the player and setting the tone.

  • Dim and directional lighting to create suspense
  • Red and dark tones to signal tension and urgency
  • Turquoise highlights to indicate teleportation points
  • Environmental clutter such as lockers, scattered papers and unorganised furniture to suggest neglect and secrecy

Each room was designed as a 360-degree VR space, encouraging players to explore their surroundings fully.


Tool and Software


  • Unity Hub – project management and version control
  • Unity Editor – scene building, lighting and interaction setup
  • C# (Unity scripting) – puzzle logic, triggers and interaction systems
  • Unity Simulator (XR Simulation) – testing gameplay without a headset
